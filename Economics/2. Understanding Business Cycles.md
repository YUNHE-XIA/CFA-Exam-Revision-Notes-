# 1. Business Cycle | 商业周期

## Definition | 定义

Business cycles are recurrent expansions and contractions in economic activity that affect broad segments of the economy.

Business cycles 是影响经济中广泛部门的反复扩张与收缩.

A cycle follows a recognizable sequence, but it is recurrent rather than periodic. Its duration, intensity, and exact pattern vary from one cycle to another.

周期具有可识别的阶段顺序, 但只是反复出现, 并非按照固定周期发生. 不同周期的持续时间, 强度和具体形态并不相同.

## Core Characteristics | 核心特征

- Expansions and contractions occur across many economic activities at approximately the same time.
- 扩张和收缩大致同时出现在许多经济活动中.
- The sequence repeats, but not at fixed intervals.
- 阶段顺序会重复, 但间隔并不固定.
- Expansions generally last longer than contractions under the classical-cycle definition.
- 按 classical cycle 定义, 扩张通常比收缩持续更长时间.
- Business cycles reflect both short-run shocks and changes in aggregate demand and aggregate supply.
- Business cycles 同时反映短期冲击以及 aggregate demand 和 aggregate supply 的变化.

## Output Gap | 产出缺口

$$
G=Y-Y^*
$$

| Symbol | Meaning | 中文含义 |
|---|---|---|
| $G$ | Output gap | 产出缺口 |
| $Y$ | Actual output | 实际产出 |
| $Y^*$ | Potential output | 潜在产出 |

| Relationship | Interpretation | 中文解释 |
|---|---|---|
| $G>0$ | Actual output exceeds potential output | 实际产出高于潜在产出 |
| $G<0$ | Actual output is below potential output | 实际产出低于潜在产出 |
| $G=0$ | Actual output equals potential output | 实际产出等于潜在产出 |

## CFA Exam Point | CFA 考试重点

- Recurrent does not mean periodic.
- Recurrent 不表示 periodic.
- A business cycle affects broad economic activity, not merely one or two industries.
- Business cycle 影响广泛经济活动, 而不仅是一个或两个行业.

# 2. Three Cycle Concepts | 三种周期概念

## Comparison | 对比

| Cycle Concept | What Fluctuates | Main Focus | Turning-Point Timing |
|---|---|---|---|
| Classical cycle | Level of real economic activity | Absolute expansion or decline in real GDP | Peaks later and troughs earlier than under the growth cycle |
| Growth cycle | Actual activity relative to potential or trend output | Positive and negative output gaps | Main framework used by many economists and practitioners |
| Growth rate cycle | Growth rate of economic activity | Acceleration and deceleration of GDP growth | Peaks and troughs usually identified earliest |

| 周期概念 | 波动对象 | 核心关注点 | 转折点时间 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Classical cycle | 实际经济活动水平 | Real GDP 的绝对扩张或下降 | 相比 growth cycle, peak 较晚且 trough 较早 |
| Growth cycle | 实际活动相对潜在或趋势产出的位置 | 正负 output gap | 许多经济学家和实务人员采用的主要框架 |
| Growth rate cycle | 经济活动增长率 | GDP 增长的加速与减速 | 通常最早识别 peak 和 trough |

## Classical Cycle | 古典周期

The classical cycle measures fluctuations in the level of real GDP. A contraction requires an absolute decline in activity.

Classical cycle 衡量 real GDP 水平的波动. Contraction 要求经济活动出现绝对下降.

It is used less frequently because absolute declines are relatively uncommon and it does not separate trend growth from short-run fluctuations.

该方法较少使用, 因为经济活动绝对下降相对少见, 并且该方法无法区分趋势增长与短期波动.

## Growth Cycle | 增长周期

The growth cycle measures actual output relative to potential output. It directly identifies whether the economy is operating above or below trend.

Growth cycle 衡量实际产出相对潜在产出的位置. 它可以直接识别经济运行高于还是低于趋势.

## Growth Rate Cycle | 增长率周期

The growth rate cycle measures acceleration and deceleration in economic growth. It does not require estimating a long-run output path.

Growth rate cycle 衡量经济增长的加速与减速. 它不需要先估计长期产出路径.

## CFA Exam Point | CFA 考试重点

- Growth can remain positive while a growth rate cycle is declining.
- 即使 growth rate cycle 正在下降, 经济增长率仍可能为正.
- Growth cycle asks whether output is above or below potential. Growth rate cycle asks whether growth is accelerating or decelerating.
- Growth cycle 关注产出高于还是低于潜在水平. Growth rate cycle 关注增长正在加速还是减速.

# 3. Four Phases of the Business Cycle | 商业周期的四个阶段

## Phase Map | 阶段图

| Phase | Output Gap | Growth | Turning-Point Position |
|---|---|---|---|
| Recovery | Most negative, then narrows | Activity begins to increase | Economy passes through the trough |
| Expansion | Opens and becomes positive | Above-average growth | Upswing and eventual boom |
| Slowdown | Largest positive gap, then narrows | Growth decelerates | Economy passes through the peak |
| Contraction | Becomes negative and opens | Below normal, possibly negative | Downturn toward the next trough |

| 阶段 | 产出缺口 | 增长 | 转折点位置 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Recovery | 负缺口最大, 随后收窄 | 经济活动开始上升 | 经济经过 trough |
| Expansion | 缺口打开并转为正值 | 增长高于平均水平 | 经济上行并最终进入 boom |
| Slowdown | 正缺口最大, 随后收窄 | 增长减速 | 经济经过 peak |
| Contraction | 缺口转负并扩大 | 增长低于正常水平, 也可能为负 | 经济下行并趋向下一个 trough |

## Recovery | 复苏

- Activity is below potential but begins to rise.
- 经济活动低于潜在水平, 但开始回升.
- The negative output gap starts to close.
- 负 output gap 开始收窄.
- Layoffs slow, overtime increases, and unemployment remains high.
- 裁员放缓, 加班增加, 失业率仍然较高.
- Inflation remains moderate.
- 通胀保持温和.

## Expansion | 扩张

- Output growth is above average and actual output rises above potential.
- 产出增长高于平均水平, 实际产出升至潜在产出以上.
- Firms move from overtime and temporary workers to permanent hiring.
- 企业从使用加班和临时员工转向正式招聘.
- Unemployment stabilizes and then falls.
- 失业率趋稳并随后下降.
- Consumer spending, production, employment, investment, credit, and profits rise.
- 消费支出, 生产, 就业, 投资, 信贷和利润上升.
- Inflation and interest rates may begin to rise.
- 通胀和利率可能开始上升.

## Boom | 繁荣

Boom is a late expansion in which growth tests the economy's capacity limits. Labor and other production factors may become scarce, wages may rise, and policy may tighten to prevent overheating.

Boom 是 expansion 的后期阶段, 此时增长正在测试经济产能极限. 劳动力和其他生产要素可能短缺, 工资可能上升, 政策可能收紧以防止经济过热.

## Slowdown | 放缓

- The positive output gap reaches its maximum and begins to narrow.
- 正 output gap 达到最大值并开始收窄.
- Activity remains high but growth decelerates.
- 经济活动仍处于高位, 但增长减速.
- Hiring continues more slowly and unemployment may still fall, but at a decreasing rate.
- 招聘继续但速度放慢, 失业率可能仍在下降, 但下降速度减慢.
- Inflation may continue accelerating because it lags the cycle.
- 由于通胀具有滞后性, 通胀可能继续加速.

## Contraction | 收缩

- Actual output falls below potential and the negative output gap opens.
- 实际产出降至潜在产出以下, 负 output gap 扩大.
- Confidence, spending, production, employment, and investment weaken.
- 信心, 支出, 生产, 就业和投资减弱.
- Firms first cut hours and overtime, then freeze hiring, and finally lay off workers.
- 企业先削减工时和加班, 随后冻结招聘, 最后裁员.
- Unemployment rises and inflation decelerates with a lag.
- 失业率上升, 通胀滞后减速.
- A severe contraction is called a depression.
- 严重的 contraction 称为 depression.

## CFA Exam Point | CFA 考试重点

- The trough is associated with the most negative output gap.
- Trough 对应最负的 output gap.
- The peak is associated with the largest positive output gap and often accelerating inflation.
- Peak 对应最大的正 output gap, 并且通常伴随通胀加速.
- Slowdown does not necessarily mean falling output. It primarily means decelerating growth.
- Slowdown 不一定表示产出下降. 它主要表示增长减速.

# 4. Financial Markets over the Cycle | 周期中的金融市场

## Typical Patterns | 典型规律

| Phase | Risky Assets | Safer Assets and Yields | Investor Behavior |
|---|---|---|---|
| Recovery | Equities and corporate credit are often repriced upward | Government bonds lose some relative appeal | Investors anticipate higher profits before economic data turn |
| Expansion | Risky assets benefit from profit and credit growth | Yields may rise as growth and inflation strengthen | Risk appetite increases |
| Slowdown | Risky assets may already have substantial gains | Government bond prices may be lower and nominal yields higher | Inflation and tightening concerns rise |
| Contraction | Cyclical risky assets weaken | Government securities and stable cash-flow companies are favored | Demand for safety and reliable income increases |

| 阶段 | 风险资产 | 安全资产与收益率 | 投资者行为 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Recovery | 股票和公司信用通常提前重估上涨 | 政府债券的相对吸引力下降 | 投资者在经济数据转强前预期利润上升 |
| Expansion | 风险资产受益于利润和信贷增长 | 随增长和通胀增强, 收益率可能上升 | 风险偏好提高 |
| Slowdown | 风险资产可能已经大幅上涨 | 政府债券价格可能较低, 名义收益率较高 | 对通胀和政策收紧的担忧增加 |
| Contraction | 周期性风险资产走弱 | 政府证券和现金流稳定企业更受青睐 | 对安全性和可靠收入的需求提高 |

Equity markets typically reach a trough approximately three to six months before the economy bottoms. Equity prices are therefore treated as a leading indicator.

股票市场通常在经济触底前约 3 至 6 个月见底. 因此 equity prices 被视为 leading indicator.

## CFA Exam Point | CFA 考试重点

Financial markets are forward looking. Market turning points can occur before reported economic indicators change direction.

金融市场具有前瞻性. 市场转折点可能早于已公布经济指标的方向变化.

# 5. Dating Recessions | 确定衰退时期

## Two-Quarter Rule | 连续两个季度规则

A common shortcut identifies a recession after two consecutive quarters of negative real GDP growth.

常用简化规则是在 real GDP 连续两个季度负增长后认定 recession.

This rule is not definitive. It may miss a broad decline when negative quarters are interrupted by one slightly positive quarter.

该规则不是最终标准. 如果两个负增长季度之间夹有一个轻微正增长季度, 它可能遗漏广泛的经济衰退.

## Business Cycle Dating Committees | 商业周期认定委员会

Organizations such as the NBER examine many series, including employment, industrial production, income, and sales. Turning points are often announced with a delay because data arrive late and are revised.

NBER 等机构会检查就业, 工业生产, 收入和销售等多项数据. 由于数据发布滞后且会修订, 周期转折点通常在较晚时间才被确认.

## CFA Exam Point | CFA 考试重点

- Two negative real GDP quarters are a practical rule, not a complete economic definition.
- Real GDP 连续两个季度负增长是实务规则, 而不是完整的经济定义.
- A broad set of indicators is more reliable than one series.
- 一组广泛指标通常比单一数据序列更可靠.

# 6. Credit Cycles | 信贷周期

## Definition | 定义

Credit cycles describe changes in the availability and pricing of credit, especially private-sector borrowing used for business investment and property purchases.

Credit cycles 描述信贷可获得性和定价的变化, 特别是用于企业投资和房地产购买的私营部门借款.

Credit cycles are part of broader financial cycles. They are connected to business cycles but are not always synchronized with them.

Credit cycles 属于更广泛的 financial cycles. 它们与 business cycles 相互关联, 但并不总是同步.

## Expansionary Credit Phase | 信贷扩张阶段

- Lenders are more willing to lend.
- 贷款机构更愿意提供信贷.
- Credit becomes more available and less expensive.
- 信贷更容易获得且成本更低.
- Borrowing, risk-taking, construction, property purchases, and asset prices increase.
- 借款, 风险承担, 建设活动, 房地产购买和资产价格上升.
- Loose credit may contribute to real estate and asset-price bubbles.
- 宽松信贷可能推动房地产和资产价格泡沫.

## Tightening Credit Phase | 信贷收紧阶段

- Lenders reduce availability and raise the price of credit.
- 贷款机构降低信贷可获得性并提高信贷成本.
- Property and other asset values may fall.
- 房地产和其他资产价值可能下降.
- Defaults increase and lender risk appetite falls further.
- 违约增加, 贷款机构的风险偏好进一步下降.
- The feedback loop can deepen economic weakness.
- 这种反馈循环可能加深经济疲弱.

## Business Cycle Interaction | 与商业周期的互动

| Combined Condition | Typical Effect |
|---|---|
| Recovery plus rapid credit, risk-taking, and house-price growth | Stronger recovery |
| Recession plus financial disruption or property and equity busts | Longer and deeper recession |

| 组合状况 | 典型影响 |
|---|---|
| Recovery 伴随信贷, 风险承担和房价快速增长 | 复苏更强劲 |
| Recession 伴随金融扰动或房地产与股票价格崩跌 | 衰退持续更久且程度更深 |

Credit cycles tend to be longer, deeper, and sharper than business cycles. Financial variables co-move closely, but their turning points need not match GDP turning points.

Credit cycles 通常比 business cycles 更长, 更深且变化更剧烈. 金融变量之间高度共同变化, 但其转折点不一定与 GDP 转折点一致.

## Measurement | 衡量方法

A composite proxy may combine variables such as the credit-to-GDP ratio and real house prices. Credit cycles often boom before recessions.

Composite proxy 可以结合 credit-to-GDP ratio 和 real house prices 等变量. Credit cycles 通常在 recession 前达到繁荣阶段.

## Policy and Investment Use | 政策与投资用途

- Assess housing, construction, and property-market conditions.
- 评估住房, 建筑和房地产市场状况.
- Assess the likely strength of expansions and severity of recessions.
- 评估扩张强度和衰退严重程度.
- Anticipate monetary, fiscal, and macroprudential policy actions.
- 预判货币, 财政和 macroprudential policy 行动.
- Identify risks from leverage, asset bubbles, and banking stress.
- 识别杠杆, 资产泡沫和银行压力风险.

Monetary and fiscal policies traditionally seek to reduce business-cycle volatility. Macroprudential policies focus more directly on limiting financial booms and systemic risk.

Monetary 和 fiscal policies 传统上致力于降低 business-cycle volatility. Macroprudential policies 更直接地限制金融繁荣和系统性风险.

Strong credit-cycle peaks are closely associated with subsequent systemic banking crises.

强劲的 credit-cycle peak 与随后发生的 systemic banking crisis 密切相关.

## CFA Exam Point | CFA 考试重点

- Credit cycles and business cycles are related, not identical.
- Credit cycles 与 business cycles 相互关联, 但并不相同.
- Loose credit can amplify expansions and later deepen contractions.
- 宽松信贷可以放大扩张, 并在之后加深收缩.
- Credit cycles are especially relevant to construction and property purchases.
- Credit cycles 与建筑活动和房地产购买尤其相关.

# 7. Employment and Resource Use | 就业与资源使用

## Employment Lags the Cycle | 就业滞后于周期

Hiring and layoffs involve search, training, and adjustment costs. Firms therefore adjust hours and overtime before changing permanent employment.

招聘和裁员涉及搜索, 培训和调整成本. 因此企业通常先调整工时和加班, 然后才改变长期就业人数.

| Phase | Employment Behavior | Capacity and Input Conditions |
|---|---|---|
| Recovery | Layoffs slow, overtime rises, unemployment stays high | Excess capacity and low input prices |
| Expansion | Temporary work and overtime give way to hiring | Capacity utilization rises and labor markets tighten |
| Slowdown | Hiring continues more slowly | Capacity is high and factor shortages may emerge |
| Contraction | Hours and overtime fall, hiring freezes, then layoffs occur | Capacity utilization falls and input-price pressure weakens |

| 阶段 | 就业行为 | 产能与投入条件 |
|---|---|---|
| Recovery | 裁员放缓, 加班增加, 失业率仍高 | 产能过剩且投入价格较低 |
| Expansion | 临时用工和加班逐步转为正式招聘 | 产能利用率上升, 劳动力市场收紧 |
| Slowdown | 招聘继续但速度放缓 | 产能利用率较高, 生产要素可能短缺 |
| Contraction | 工时和加班下降, 招聘冻结, 随后裁员 | 产能利用率下降, 投入价格压力减弱 |

## CFA Exam Point | CFA 考试重点

- Employment is generally a lagging indicator.
- Employment 通常是 lagging indicator.
- Firms first alter hours and overtime, then employment.
- 企业先调整工时和加班, 随后调整就业人数.

# 8. Capital Spending | 资本支出

## Cyclical Behavior | 周期行为

Business investment is one of the most procyclical and volatile components of GDP. It depends on profits, cash flow, expectations, interest rates, and capacity utilization.

Business investment 是 GDP 中最顺周期且波动最大的组成部分之一. 它取决于利润, 现金流, 预期, 利率和产能利用率.

| Phase | Capital Spending Pattern | Typical Orders |
|---|---|---|
| Recovery | Low but rising, with focus on efficiency | Software, systems, and light equipment with high obsolescence |
| Expansion | Shifts toward capacity expansion | Heavy equipment, warehouses, and factories |
| Slowdown | Remains high but may reflect overinvestment | Capacity-related orders continue near the peak |
| Contraction | New orders stop and existing orders are canceled | Technology and light equipment cut first, heavy equipment and construction later |

| 阶段 | 资本支出规律 | 典型订单 |
|---|---|---|
| Recovery | 水平较低但开始上升, 重点是提高效率 | 软件, 系统和淘汰速度较快的轻型设备 |
| Expansion | 转向扩大产能 | 重型设备, 仓库和工厂 |
| Slowdown | 仍处于高位, 但可能反映过度投资 | 接近 peak 时继续下达扩产订单 |
| Contraction | 停止新订单并取消现有订单 | 先削减技术和轻型设备, 后削减重型设备和建筑项目 |

New orders lead actual shipments and spending. Analysts often examine core orders that exclude defense and aircraft because large, long-delivery items can distort the underlying trend.

New orders 领先于实际交付和支出. 分析师通常观察剔除国防和飞机的 core orders, 因为大型且交付周期较长的项目可能扭曲基础趋势.

## CFA Exam Point | CFA 考试重点

- Equipment orders are easier to cancel than large construction projects, so they usually decline first in a contraction.
- 设备订单比大型建筑项目更容易取消, 因此通常在 contraction 中率先下降.
- Orders are more forward looking than shipments.
- Orders 比 shipments 更具前瞻性.

# 9. Inventories | 存货

## Inventory-Sales Ratio | 存货销售比率

$$
ISR=\frac{I}{S}
$$

| Symbol | Meaning | 中文含义 |
|---|---|---|
| $ISR$ | Inventory-sales ratio | 存货销售比率 |
| $I$ | Inventories | 存货 |
| $S$ | Sales | 销售额 |

Inventories are small relative to total economic output, but rapid inventory accumulation and liquidation can materially affect measured GDP growth.

存货相对总经济产出规模较小, 但快速的存货积累与清理可以显著影响 GDP 增长.

## Cycle Pattern | 周期规律

| Phase | Sales, Production, and Inventory | Inventory-Sales Ratio |
|---|---|---|
| Recovery | Sales recover before production, so unwanted inventories are liquidated | Falls to low levels |
| Expansion | Production rises to meet sales and rebuild inventories | Low, then stabilizes toward normal |
| Slowdown | Sales slow faster than production, causing inventory accumulation | Rises and reaches high levels near the peak |
| Contraction | Production is cut below sales to dispose of unwanted inventories | Falls back toward normal |

| 阶段 | 销售, 生产与存货 | Inventory-Sales Ratio |
|---|---|---|
| Recovery | 销售先于生产复苏, 因此企业清理不需要的存货 | 下降至较低水平 |
| Expansion | 生产上升以满足销售并重建存货 | 处于低位, 随后趋向正常水平 |
| Slowdown | 销售减速快于生产, 导致存货积累 | 上升并在 peak 附近达到高位 |
| Contraction | 企业将生产降至销售以下以清理不需要的存货 | 回落至正常水平 |

## CFA Exam Point | CFA 考试重点

- Inventories often fall early in recovery because sales outpace production.
- Recovery 初期存货通常下降, 因为销售增长快于生产.
- A rising inventory-sales ratio near the top of the cycle signals weakening demand.
- 周期顶部附近 inventory-sales ratio 上升表示需求正在减弱.
- In contraction, firms may reduce equipment maintenance rather than sell physical capital immediately.
- 在 contraction 中, 企业可能减少设备维护, 而不是立即出售实物资本.

# 10. Consumer Activity | 消费者活动

## Typical Cycle Pattern | 典型周期规律

| Phase | Consumer Behavior | Useful Indicators |
|---|---|---|
| Recovery | Confidence and spending begin to improve from low levels | Consumer confidence, retail sales, and credit use |
| Expansion | Income, employment, borrowing, and discretionary spending rise | Retail sales, auto sales, personal income, and consumer credit |
| Slowdown | Confidence remains positive but spending growth decelerates | Durable-goods demand and confidence often weaken first |
| Contraction | Employment insecurity reduces discretionary and durable-goods spending | Falling retail and auto sales, weaker confidence, and tighter credit |

| 阶段 | 消费者行为 | 常用指标 |
|---|---|---|
| Recovery | 信心和支出从低位开始改善 | Consumer confidence, retail sales 和 credit use |
| Expansion | 收入, 就业, 借款和可选消费支出上升 | Retail sales, auto sales, personal income 和 consumer credit |
| Slowdown | 信心仍然较强, 但支出增长减速 | Durable-goods demand 和 confidence 通常率先走弱 |
| Contraction | 就业不安全降低可选消费和耐用品支出 | Retail 和 auto sales 下降, confidence 走弱且信贷收紧 |

Durable goods are generally more cyclical than essential goods and services because purchases can be postponed.

Durable goods 通常比必需品和服务更具周期性, 因为其购买可以推迟.

## CFA Exam Point | CFA 考试重点

Confidence measures may lead actual spending, while employment and wage measures often lag.

Confidence measures 可能领先实际支出, 而 employment 和 wage measures 通常滞后.

# 11. Housing Activity | 住房活动

## Typical Cycle Pattern | 典型周期规律

Housing is highly cyclical, interest-rate sensitive, and closely linked to credit availability. It often turns before broader economic activity.

Housing 具有高度周期性和利率敏感性, 并与信贷可获得性密切相关. 它通常早于整体经济活动转向.

| Phase | Housing Activity | Useful Indicators |
|---|---|---|
| Recovery | Lower rates and easier credit support demand | Building permits, housing starts, and home sales rise |
| Expansion | Construction, transactions, and prices strengthen | Starts, permits, mortgage lending, and construction employment |
| Slowdown | Higher rates and reduced affordability weaken activity | Permits and new home sales decline before completed construction |
| Contraction | Tight credit, weaker income, and falling prices reduce activity | Starts, permits, transactions, and construction employment remain weak |

| 阶段 | 住房活动 | 常用指标 |
|---|---|---|
| Recovery | 较低利率和更宽松信贷支持需求 | Building permits, housing starts 和 home sales 上升 |
| Expansion | 建筑, 交易和价格增强 | Starts, permits, mortgage lending 和 construction employment |
| Slowdown | 较高利率和可负担性下降削弱活动 | Permits 和 new home sales 早于完工项目下降 |
| Contraction | 信贷收紧, 收入走弱和价格下降压低活动 | Starts, permits, transactions 和 construction employment 保持疲弱 |

## CFA Exam Point | CFA 考试重点

Residential building permits are leading indicators because authorization occurs before construction and completion.

Residential building permits 是 leading indicators, 因为审批发生在施工和完工之前.

# 12. External Trade | 对外贸易

## Typical Cycle Pattern | 典型周期规律

External trade depends on the domestic cycle, foreign cycles, relative prices, and exchange rates. Domestic and foreign economies do not necessarily turn at the same time.

External trade 取决于国内周期, 国外周期, 相对价格和汇率. 国内外经济不一定同时转向.

| Domestic Phase | Imports | Trade Balance Tendency |
|---|---|---|
| Recovery and expansion | Rise as domestic income and spending strengthen | May deteriorate if imports grow faster than exports |
| Slowdown and contraction | Weaken as domestic demand falls | May improve if imports fall faster than exports |

| 国内阶段 | 进口 | 贸易余额倾向 |
|---|---|---|
| Recovery 和 expansion | 随国内收入和支出增强而上升 | 如果进口增长快于出口, 贸易余额可能恶化 |
| Slowdown 和 contraction | 随国内需求下降而走弱 | 如果进口下降快于出口, 贸易余额可能改善 |

Exports depend primarily on economic conditions in trading partners. Strong foreign growth can support domestic exports even when the domestic economy is weak.

Exports 主要取决于贸易伙伴的经济状况. 即使国内经济疲弱, 强劲的国外增长仍可能支持国内出口.

## CFA Exam Point | CFA 考试重点

Do not infer the trade balance from the domestic cycle alone. Foreign demand and exchange rates also matter.

不要只根据国内周期推断贸易余额. 国外需求和汇率同样重要.

# 13. Economic Indicators | 经济指标

## Definition | 定义

Economic indicators are statistics that provide information about an economy's recent past, current condition, or likely future position in the business cycle.

Economic indicators 是提供经济近期过去, 当前状况或未来可能周期位置的信息统计数据.

## Timing Classification | 时间分类

| Indicator Type | Turning Point | Main Use | 中文解释 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Leading | Usually before the overall economy | Predict the near-term future | 通常领先整体经济, 用于预测近期状态 |

| Lagging | Usually after the overall economy | Confirm the past state or established trend | 通常滞后于整体经济, 用于确认过去状态或已经形成的趋势 |

These classifications are based on observed historical relationships. The exact indicators differ across countries and may change over time.

这些分类基于历史观察到的关系. 具体指标因国家而异, 并且可能随时间变化.

|---|---|---|

- Leading 不表示能够完美预测.

- 应使用多项指标并寻找相互确认, 而不是依赖单一数据发布.

# 16. Diffusion Index | 扩散指数

## Definition | 定义

A diffusion index measures the breadth of movement in a composite index. It reflects the proportion of components moving in a direction consistent with the overall index.

Diffusion index 衡量 composite index 变化的广度. 它反映与整体指数方向一致的组成部分比例.

## Conference Board Scoring Example | Conference Board 计分示例

| Component Change | Assigned Score |
|---|---:|
| Rises by more than 0.05% | 1.0 |
| Changes by less than 0.05% in absolute terms | 0.5 |
| Falls by more than 0.05% | 0 |

$$
DI=\frac{\sum_{i=1}^{N}S_i}{N}\times100
$$

| Symbol | Meaning | 中文含义 |
|---|---|---|
| $DI$ | Diffusion index | 扩散指数 |
| $S_i$ | Assigned score for Component $i$ | 第 $i$ 个组成部分的分值 |
| $N$ | Number of components | 组成部分数量 |

## Month 1 Example | 第一个月例题

Scores are 1.0, 1.0, 0.5, and 0.

分值为 1.0, 1.0, 0.5 和 0.

$$
DI=\frac{1.0+1.0+0.5+0}{4}\times100=62.5
$$

## Month 2 Example | 第二个月例题

Scores are 0, 1.0, 1.0, and 1.0.

分值为 0, 1.0, 1.0 和 1.0.

$$
DI=\frac{0+1.0+1.0+1.0}{4}\times100=75
$$

The increase from 62.5 to 75 means that more components are rising. It provides broader confirmation of the composite index movement.

从 62.5 上升至 75 表示更多组成部分正在上升. 它为 composite index 的变化提供更广泛确认.

## CFA Exam Point | CFA 考试重点

- A diffusion index measures breadth, not the magnitude of economic growth.
- Diffusion index 衡量变化广度, 而不是经济增长幅度.
- It reduces the influence of an extreme change in one component.
- 它可以降低单个组成部分极端变化的影响.

# 17. Using Indicators to Identify a Cycle Phase | 使用指标识别周期阶段

## Confirmation Framework | 确认框架

1. Examine leading indicators for an early signal.
2. Check whether other leading indicators confirm the direction.
3. Use coincident indicators to verify current economic activity.
4. Use lagging indicators as evidence that the trend has been underway.
5. Reassess when indicators conflict or data are revised.

1. 使用 leading indicators 寻找早期信号.
2. 检查其他 leading indicators 是否确认相同方向.
3. 使用 coincident indicators 验证当前经济活动.
4. 使用 lagging indicators 证明趋势已经持续一段时间.
5. 当指标冲突或数据修订时重新评估.

## Example Interpretation | 示例解释

| Observation | Classification | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|
| Equity index rises | Leading | Possible future recovery |
| Industrial production and non-farm payrolls rise | Coincident | Current activity is improving |
| Consumer installment debt relative to income rises | Lagging | An upturn has already been underway |

| 观察结果 | 分类 | 解释 |
|---|---|---|
| Equity index 上升 | Leading | 未来可能 recovery |
| Industrial production 和 non-farm payrolls 上升 | Coincident | 当前经济活动正在改善 |
| Consumer installment debt relative to income 上升 | Lagging | Upturn 已经持续一段时间 |

## Peak Signal Example | Peak 信号示例

Narrowing yield spreads, declining capital-goods orders, falling residential permits, payrolls turning down, stable sales, and rising unit labor costs together are most consistent with a business-cycle peak.

收益率利差收窄, 资本品订单下降, 住宅许可下降, payrolls 转为下降, 销售稳定以及 unit labor costs 上升, 这些信号共同最符合 business-cycle peak.

## CFA Exam Point | CFA 考试重点

An increase in one indicator, such as average hourly earnings, is not enough to establish a turning point. Seek confirmation from other indicators.

单一指标上升, 例如 average hourly earnings 上升, 不足以确认周期转折点. 应从其他指标寻找确认.

# CFA Memory Points | CFA 记忆重点

## 1. Cycle Definitions | 周期定义

- Classical cycle: level of activity.
- Classical cycle: 经济活动水平.
- Growth cycle: output relative to potential.
- Growth cycle: 产出相对潜在水平的位置.
- Growth rate cycle: acceleration or deceleration of growth.
- Growth rate cycle: 增长加速或减速.

## 2. Four Phases | 四个阶段

`Recovery -> Expansion -> Slowdown -> Contraction`.

`Recovery -> Expansion -> Slowdown -> Contraction`.

Recovery begins at the trough. Slowdown begins near the peak.

Recovery 从 trough 开始. Slowdown 从 peak 附近开始.

## 3. Output Gap | 产出缺口

$$
G=Y-Y^*
$$

Recovery narrows a negative gap. Expansion opens a positive gap. Slowdown narrows a positive gap. Contraction opens a negative gap.

Recovery 收窄负缺口. Expansion 打开正缺口. Slowdown 收窄正缺口. Contraction 打开负缺口.

## 4. Employment | 就业

Employment lags the cycle. Firms change hours and overtime before changing headcount.

Employment 滞后于周期. 企业先调整工时和加班, 再调整就业人数.

## 5. Capital Spending | 资本支出

Investment is procyclical and volatile. Orders lead shipments. Light equipment is canceled before heavy construction.

Investment 顺周期且波动较大. Orders 领先 shipments. 轻型设备订单早于重型建筑项目被取消.

## 6. Inventories | 存货

Sales recover before production, so inventories fall early in recovery. Sales slow before production, so the inventory-sales ratio rises near the peak.

销售先于生产复苏, 因此 recovery 初期存货下降. 销售先于生产减速, 因此 inventory-sales ratio 在 peak 附近上升.

## 7. Credit Cycle | 信贷周期

Credit cycles are usually longer, deeper, and sharper than business cycles. Strong credit peaks can precede banking crises.

Credit cycles 通常比 business cycles 更长, 更深且变化更剧烈. 强劲的 credit peak 可能领先 banking crisis.

## 8. Indicator Timing | 指标时间关系

- Leading predicts.
- Leading 用于预测.
- Coincident identifies the present.
- Coincident 用于识别当前状态.
- Lagging confirms the past trend.
- Lagging 用于确认过去趋势.

## 9. Diffusion Index | 扩散指数

$$
DI=\frac{\sum_{i=1}^{N}S_i}{N}\times100
$$

Diffusion index measures breadth, not growth magnitude.

Diffusion index 衡量变化广度, 而不是增长幅度.

## 10. Common Mistakes | 常见错误

- Recurrent does not mean periodic.
- Recurrent 不表示 periodic.
- Slowdown does not necessarily mean negative growth.
- Slowdown 不一定表示负增长.
- Credit and business cycles are related but not synchronized.
- Credit cycle 与 business cycle 相互关联, 但不一定同步.
- One indicator does not establish a turning point.
- 单一指标不能确认周期转折点.

## 11. Exam Shortcut | 考试技巧

When identifying a phase, read indicators in this order: leading signals, coincident confirmation, then lagging evidence.

识别周期阶段时, 按以下顺序阅读指标: leading signals, coincident confirmation, 然后是 lagging evidence.
