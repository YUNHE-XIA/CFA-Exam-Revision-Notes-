# Analyzing Balance Sheets | 资产负债表分析

## 1. 核心记忆

Balance sheet 披露公司在某一个具体时间点的财务状况：

- Assets：公司拥有什么；
- Liabilities：公司欠什么；
- Equity：股东对净资产的剩余权益。

基本公式：

$$
Assets = Liabilities + Equity
$$

资产负债表分析的核心目的：

- 评估 liquidity；
- 评估 solvency；
- 理解资产质量；
- 理解资本结构；
- 比较不同公司的 financial position。

记忆：

> Income statement 看一段时间的经营结果。  
> Balance sheet 看某一时点的财务状况。

---

## 2. Measurement Bases | 计量基础

Balance sheet 上不同项目可能采用不同 measurement basis。

| Measurement Basis | 中文理解 |
|---|---|
| Historical cost | 历史成本 |
| Amortized cost | 摊余成本 |
| Fair value | 公允价值 |
| Lower of cost or NRV | 成本与可变现净值孰低 |

重要理解：

> 资产负债表不是所有项目都按 market value 计量。  
> Analyst 需要结合 notes 判断 comparability。

---

## 3. Intangible Assets | 无形资产

Intangible assets 是：

> identifiable non-monetary assets without physical substance.

常见例子：

- patents；
- licenses；
- trademarks；
- copyrights；
- customer lists；
- franchises。

关键点：

> Intangible asset 必须 identifiable。  
> Goodwill 通常不是 separately identifiable intangible asset。

---

## 4. Finite-Life vs Indefinite-Life Intangibles

### Finite-life intangible assets

有限寿命无形资产需要 amortize。

处理：

- amortized over useful life；
- amortization method reviewed at least annually；
- subject to impairment test。

记忆：

> Finite life = amortization + impairment.

### Indefinite-life intangible assets

无限寿命无形资产不 amortize。

处理：

- not amortized；
- tested for impairment at least annually；
- review whether indefinite-life assumption remains reasonable。

记忆：

> Indefinite life = no amortization + annual impairment test.

---

## 5. Internally Generated Intangibles

内部产生的 intangible 是考试高频点。

### IFRS

IFRS 区分：

- research phase；
- development phase。

Research phase：

> expense immediately.

Development phase：

> may be capitalized if criteria are met.

记忆：

> IFRS: Research expense, development may capitalize.

### US GAAP

US GAAP 通常要求：

> most R&D costs are expensed.

常见不能资本化的项目：

- internally generated brands；
- customer lists；
- start-up costs；
- training costs；
- advertising and promotion；
- relocation costs；
- reorganization costs。

记忆：

> US GAAP: most internally generated intangibles and R&D are expensed.

---

## 6. Acquired Intangibles

Purchased or acquired intangible assets can be capitalized if they are identifiable.

例如：

- purchased patent；
- acquired license；
- acquired customer list；
- acquired trademark；
- franchise right。

记忆：

> Purchased identifiable intangible can be capitalized.  
> Internally generated intangible usually cannot.

---

## 7. Goodwill

Goodwill arises only in business combinations.

$$
Goodwill = Purchase\ Price - Fair\ Value\ of\ Net\ Identifiable\ Assets
$$

$$
Net\ Identifiable\ Assets = Fair\ Value\ of\ Identifiable\ Assets - Fair\ Value\ of\ Liabilities
$$

Goodwill 代表：

- reputation；
- synergies；
- customer loyalty；
- distribution network；
- expected excess returns。

记忆：

> Goodwill exists on the balance sheet only when acquired in a business combination.

---

## 8. Accounting Goodwill vs Economic Goodwill

### Accounting goodwill

- arises from acquisition；
- reported on balance sheet；
- based on accounting rules；
- not amortized；
- tested annually for impairment。

### Economic goodwill

- based on company’s real economic advantage；
- reflected in stock price；
- may not appear on balance sheet；
- can exist even without acquisition。

记忆：

> Accounting goodwill comes from acquisition.  
> Economic goodwill comes from economic performance.

---

## 9. Goodwill Impairment

Goodwill is not amortized.

Instead:

> tested for impairment at least annually.

If impaired:

- impairment loss reduces net income；
- total assets decrease；
- equity decreases；
- non-cash expense；
- future ROA may increase because asset base becomes smaller。

记忆：

> Goodwill impairment lowers current earnings but does not directly use cash.

---

## 10. Financial Instruments

Financial instrument 是一种 contract，会同时产生：

- financial asset for one entity；
- financial liability or equity instrument for another entity。

常见 financial assets：

- stocks held as investment；
- bonds held as investment；
- notes receivable；
- loans receivable；
- derivatives。

常见 financial liabilities：

- bonds payable；
- notes payable；
- loans payable；
- derivatives with negative value。

---

## 11. Financial Asset Measurement Categories

Financial assets 一般按两种方式计量：

- amortized cost；
- fair value。

Fair value 的 unrealized gains/losses 又分为：

- through profit or loss；
- through OCI。

---

## 12. Amortized Cost / Held-to-Maturity

适用于：

- debt securities；
- cash flows are solely principal and interest；
- company intends to hold to maturity；
- business model is collecting contractual cash flows。

处理：

- balance sheet at amortized cost；
- interest income recognized in income statement；
- unrealized gains/losses usually not recognized。

记忆：

> Held-to-maturity debt securities are measured at amortized cost.

---

## 13. Fair Value Through OCI

适用于某些 debt securities。

处理：

- balance sheet at fair value；
- interest income goes to income statement；
- unrealized gains/losses go to OCI；
- unrealized gains/losses bypass income statement。

US GAAP 类似：

> available-for-sale debt securities.

记忆：

> FVOCI affects equity through OCI, not net income.

---

## 14. Fair Value Through Profit or Loss

适用于：

- trading securities；
- assets not classified into other categories；
- many equity securities；
- financial assets designated at FVTPL。

处理：

- balance sheet at fair value；
- unrealized gains/losses go to income statement；
- net income becomes more volatile。

记忆：

> FVTPL affects both balance sheet and income statement.

---

## 15. IFRS vs US GAAP: Financial Instruments

### IFRS

Financial assets may be:

- amortized cost；
- fair value through OCI；
- fair value through profit or loss。

IFRS allows some equity investments to be measured at FVOCI by irrevocable election.

### US GAAP

- held-to-maturity debt: amortized cost；
- available-for-sale debt: fair value through OCI；
- trading debt: fair value through income；
- most equity securities: fair value through income。

记忆：

> US GAAP does not allow equity securities to be classified as available-for-sale in the same way as debt securities.

---

## 16. Non-Current Liabilities

Non-current liabilities are liabilities not due within one year or one operating cycle.

Common examples:

- long-term loans；
- bonds payable；
- notes payable；
- lease liabilities；
- deferred tax liabilities；
- pension liabilities；
- non-current deferred revenue。

---

## 17. Long-Term Financial Liabilities

Bonds payable and loans payable are usually reported at amortized cost.

If issued at par:

> carrying amount = face value.

If issued at discount:

> carrying amount initially below face value and increases toward face value over time.

If issued at premium:

> carrying amount initially above face value and decreases toward face value over time.

记忆：

> At maturity, bond carrying amount equals face value.

---

## 18. Deferred Tax Liabilities

Deferred tax liability arises when:

> taxable income is lower than accounting income now, so taxes paid now are lower than tax expense, but taxes will be paid later.

常见原因：

- accelerated depreciation for tax；
- straight-line depreciation for accounting；
- income recognized earlier in accounting than in tax。

记忆：

> DTL means tax paid later.

---

## 19. Common-Size Balance Sheet

Vertical common-size balance sheet expresses each item as a percentage of total assets.

$$
Common\ Size\ Item = \frac{Balance\ Sheet\ Item}{Total\ Assets}
$$

用途：

- compare companies of different sizes；
- analyze asset composition；
- analyze liability structure；
- compare across time；
- compare with industry peers。

记忆：

> Balance sheet common-size uses total assets as 100%.

---

## 20. Liquidity

Liquidity measures a company’s ability to meet short-term obligations.

核心问题：

> Can the company pay near-term liabilities?

Liquidity analysis focuses on:

- cash；
- marketable securities；
- receivables；
- inventory；
- current liabilities。

---

## 21. Liquidity Ratios

### Current Ratio

$$
Current\ Ratio = \frac{Current\ Assets}{Current\ Liabilities}
$$

Meaning:

> ability to meet current liabilities using current assets.

### Quick Ratio / Acid-Test Ratio

$$
Quick\ Ratio =
\frac{Cash + Marketable\ Securities + Receivables}{Current\ Liabilities}
$$

Meaning:

> stricter liquidity measure because inventory is excluded.

### Cash Ratio

$$
Cash\ Ratio =
\frac{Cash + Marketable\ Securities}{Current\ Liabilities}
$$

Meaning:

> most conservative liquidity ratio.

记忆：

> Current ratio includes inventory.  
> Quick ratio excludes inventory.  
> Cash ratio only uses cash and marketable securities.

---

## 22. Solvency

Solvency measures a company’s ability to meet long-term obligations.

核心问题：

> Is the company too leveraged?

Solvency analysis focuses on:

- long-term debt；
- total debt；
- total liabilities；
- equity；
- capital structure。

---

## 23. Solvency Ratios

### Long-Term Debt-to-Equity

$$
Long\text{-}term\ Debt\text{-}to\text{-}Equity =
\frac{Long\text{-}term\ Debt}{Total\ Equity}
$$

Meaning:

> long-term financial leverage.

### Debt-to-Equity

$$
Debt\text{-}to\text{-}Equity =
\frac{Total\ Debt}{Total\ Equity}
$$

Meaning:

> financial risk and leverage.

### Debt-to-Assets

$$
Debt\text{-}to\text{-}Assets =
\frac{Total\ Debt}{Total\ Assets}
$$

Meaning:

> percentage of assets financed by debt.

### Financial Leverage

$$
Financial\ Leverage =
\frac{Total\ Assets}{Total\ Equity}
$$

Meaning:

> how much assets are supported by each dollar of equity.

记忆：

> Higher leverage means higher financial risk.

---

## 24. Ratio Analysis Limitations

Ratios are useful but not perfect.

Limitations:

- accounting methods differ across companies；
- diversified companies are hard to compare；
- industry structure matters；
- ratios may reflect temporary conditions；
- current ratio does not measure quality of current assets；
- inventory may not be easily converted to cash；
- receivables may have collection risk；
- off-balance-sheet commitments may be ignored。

记忆：

> Ratio is a starting point, not the final answer.

---

## 25. Analyst Adjustments

Analysts may adjust balance sheet data by:

- removing goodwill for tangible book value；
- excluding goodwill impairment from recurring earnings；
- comparing fair value and amortized cost disclosures；
- adjusting for off-balance-sheet commitments；
- evaluating intangible asset quality；
- separating operating liabilities from financing liabilities。

---

## 26. Exam Traps | 高频陷阱

- Balance sheet is at a point in time, not over a period.
- Not all assets are measured at fair value.
- Notes are essential for comparability.
- Finite-life intangible assets are amortized.
- Indefinite-life intangible assets are not amortized.
- Goodwill is not amortized.
- Goodwill is tested annually for impairment.
- Internally generated goodwill is not recognized.
- Accounting goodwill arises only from acquisitions.
- IFRS: research costs expensed, development costs may be capitalized.
- US GAAP: most internally generated intangibles and R&D are expensed.
- Acquired identifiable intangibles are capitalized.
- Goodwill impairment is non-cash but reduces net income.
- Amortized cost securities do not recognize unrealized gains/losses.
- FVOCI unrealized gains/losses go to OCI.
- FVTPL unrealized gains/losses go to income statement.
- Current ratio includes inventory.
- Quick ratio excludes inventory.
- Cash ratio is the most conservative liquidity ratio.
- Common-size balance sheet uses total assets as denominator.
- High current ratio does not always mean strong liquidity.
- High leverage increases solvency risk.

---

## 27. Quick Review | 考前速记

- Balance sheet = assets, liabilities, equity at one point in time.
- Assets = Liabilities + Equity.
- Liquidity = ability to meet short-term obligations.
- Solvency = ability to meet long-term obligations.
- Intangible asset = identifiable, non-monetary, no physical substance.
- Finite-life intangible = amortize.
- Indefinite-life intangible = no amortization, impairment test.
- Goodwill = purchase price minus fair value of net identifiable assets.
- Goodwill only arises from acquisition.
- Goodwill impairment reduces income and assets.
- IFRS research expense, development may capitalize.
- US GAAP usually expenses internally generated intangibles and R&D.
- Financial instruments may be measured at amortized cost or fair value.
- FVTPL affects net income.
- FVOCI affects OCI, not net income.
- Held-to-maturity debt uses amortized cost.
- Long-term liabilities usually include bonds, loans, leases, deferred taxes.
- Deferred tax liability means taxes payable in future.
- Common-size balance sheet = item / total assets.
- Current ratio = current assets / current liabilities.
- Quick ratio = cash + marketable securities + receivables / current liabilities.
- Cash ratio = cash + marketable securities / current liabilities.
- Financial leverage = total assets / total equity.
